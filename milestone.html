Rain It In – Milestone Report

Accomplishments

We’ve implemented the core 2D rain‐droplet simulation:

  • Particle dynamics under gravity (fixed‐timestep integration).
  • Collision detection with a slanted platform.
  • Splash generation using Gaussian and log‐normal distributions.
  • Sliding behavior along slopes with friction.
  • Merging of resting splash particles into puddles.
  • Matplotlib‐based animation over 200 frames.

Preliminary Results

The simulation animates raindrops falling, splashing on impact, then sliding or merging into puddles. Initial tests run smoothly at ~20–30 FPS for 200 frames on our development machines.

Reflection & Updated Work Plan

We completed our Week 1 goal of a working particle system with splash and slide physics on schedule. Next, we’ll shift focus to user interaction and extended features. Below is our updated schedule:

Week Tasks
Week 2 Build UI controls (rain intensity slider, droplet‐size selector) and parameter tuning interfaces.
Week 3 Implement pooling/puddling on flat areas, add optional obstacles (e.g. mouse as boundary object).
Week 4 Add wind simulation (direction & strength), polish rendering, and optimize performance.
